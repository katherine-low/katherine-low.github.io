Top 3 Dishes in South Korea

Naengmyeon

Naengmyeon is a South Korean dish that consists of cold buckwheat noodles in a chilled meat or kimchi broth. The cold soup provides a refreshing meal to cool off from the heat, so it is typically eaten in the summer. Learn how to make naengmyeon here

Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is an iconic South Korean street food made of chewy rice cakes and fish cakes in a spicy and sweet sauce made with gochujang (red chili paste). The word tteokbokki comes from the Korean words tteok, meaning "rice cake," and bokki, meaning "stir-fried."

Bingsu

Bingsu is a Korean milk-based shaved ice dessert. It is topped with many sweet toppings including chopped fruits, like mangoes or strawberries, condensed milk, and red beans. It is popular during the summertime, as it is a cool treat during the hot summer months.
